Location:

Taiwan is located in the eastern part of Asia, at the intersection of Northeast Asia and Southeast Asia. Its relative positions with the surrounding areas are: east to the Pacific Ocean (Philippine Sea), west to the Taiwan Strait and Eurasia. From the perspective of geopolitical theory, Taiwan is located exactly in the central area of the East Asian island arc and is an important hub and strategic location for Asia-Pacific economic and trade transportation.

    Climate:

    The climate of Taiwan Island has a subtropical monsoon climate to the north of the Tropic of Cancer and a tropical monsoon climate to the south. Or divide the whole territory into tropical and subtropical zones, and use the northern and southern boundaries of the island as a transition zone. In addition, although it is a subtropical island with a warm flat climate, the mountainous area of Taiwan Island is full of temperate plants due to the extremely high mountains. In winter, the snow-capped mountains in the north even snow every year.

    Population:

    The population of Taiwan is approximately 23.57 million, spread across a total land area of about 36,000 km2; it is the seventeenth most densely populated country in the world, with a population density of about 651 inhabitants per square kilometer.
    The original population of the island of Taiwan and its associated islands consists of a variety of Taiwanese indigenous peoples.They share the same ancestory DNA with the Austronesian family and with island peoples of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.
    Immigration of Han Chinese to the Penghu islands started as early as the 13th century, while settlement of the main island occurred from the 16th century. It was stimulated by Dutch traders importing workers from Fujian in the 17th century. According to governmental statistics, in the early 21st century, more than 95% of the Republic of China's population are Han Chinese of East Asian ethnicity, while 2.3% are Taiwanese aborigines of Austronesian ethnicity. 85% of Taiwanese have some level of aboriginal ancestry.

    Information taken from wikipedia

    Language:

    The languages of Taiwan consist of several varieties of languages under families of Austronesian languages and Sino-Tibetan languages spoken in Taiwan.
    Researches on historical linguistics recognize Taiwan as the homeland of the whole Austronesian languages family owing to the highest internal variety of the Formosan languages.
    In the last 400 years, several waves of Chinese emigrations brought several different Sino-Tibetan languages into Taiwan. These languages include traditional Mandarin known as the national language, Taiwanese Hokkien, spoken by about 70% of the population and Hakka in Taiwan.

    Culture:

    Business:

    Taiwan maintains a stable industrial economy as a result of rapid economic growth and industrialization. The 21st-largest economy in the world, its high-tech industry plays a key role in the global economy. Worldwilde known Cooperations includes such as Foxconn, Acer, Asus, and Giant. Taiwan is ranked highly in terms of freedom of the press, health care, public education, economic freedom, and human development. According to the 2019 Expat Insider survey, Taiwan is ranked as the best destination for expat for its high quality of life, good personal finances, and great working life. It is rated best in the world for the affordability of healthcare, with 89 percent of respondents satisfied with this factor (versus 55 percent globally). In addition to the logistical aspects of life, 88 percent of expats find that Taiwanese locals are generally friendly.

    Taiwanese Cuisine

    Taiwanese cuisine in a broad sense refers to dishes created in Taiwan or in a narrow sense it is limited to traditional Taiwanese southern Hokkien dishes. Although similar to traditional Chinese cuisine, the very same ingredients are not available in Taiwan. Along time the local cuisine has developed into a variety of innovative cuisines influenced by Fujian, Hakka, Aboriginal people, other provinces of China, Japanese and many other foreign countries. The worldknown bubble milk tea was born in this island.
